# THREAD_ARCHAEOLOGY

## Core identity
Forums, screenshots, comments, timestamps, and repost strata as excavation sites.

## Core vibe
nested screenshots, thread sediment, avatar ghosts, cropping lineage, metadata scars, archive melancholy

## Visual markers
- dense layering
- internet-native symbols
- visible mediation by platforms or interfaces
- emotional contradiction
- some degree of crude or damaged image logic

## Materials / texture
- low-res screenshot residue
- browser chrome
- compression or glow behavior
- sticker-like overlays
- social trace

## Common motifs
- UI fragments
- tags, captions, icons, badges, windows, buttons, or relic objects appropriate to the branch
- repeated or recycled image matter
- archive evidence

## Palette tendencies
Depends on branch, but usually stronger when the palette feels platform-native rather than painterly.

## Composition behavior
- additive
- accumulative
- modular
- tolerant of clutter
- often recursive or self-referential

## Prompt language
nested screenshots, thread sediment, avatar ghosts, cropping lineage, metadata scars, archive melancholy

## Good pairings
- hyperpop
- weirdcore
- emo/scene
- op art
- vaporwave
- corporate decay
- sacred digital iconography

## Negatives
- polished modern startup aesthetics
- luxury minimal restraint
- clean contemporary brand illustration
- frictionless “nice” design

## Prompt snippet
A scene rendered through the logic of thread archaeology, with visible interface matter, social residue, and image-history scars, dense composition, internet-native symbolism, and no attempt to tidy the weirdness away.
